# AI Setup Call

The AI Setup Call is a crucial part of your Ravan.ai onboarding process. This 30-45 minute call with our AI helps configure your agent to perfectly match your business needs and use cases.

## Why an AI Setup Call?

Unlike traditional software setup, Ravan.ai uses an AI Setup Call to:

- Understand your specific business context
- Configure your agent's personality and expertise
- Set up initial use cases and workflows
- Ensure optimal performance from day one

## Preparing for Your Setup Call

### Before the Call
1. **Gather Information**
   - Business goals and use cases
   - Target audience details
   - Current pain points
   - Desired outcomes

2. **Prepare Materials**
   - Example conversations (if available)
   - Brand guidelines
   - Key terminology
   - Common customer questions

3. **Schedule the Call**
   - Choose a quiet environment
   - Block 45 minutes
   - Have a stable internet connection

### During the Call
1. **Initial Assessment**
   - Business overview
   - Use case discussion
   - Goals and expectations

2. **Personality Configuration**
   - Tone and style preferences
   - Expertise level
   - Response patterns

3. **Use Case Setup**
   - Conversation flows
   - Key information gathering
   - Integration requirements

4. **Testing and Validation**
   - Sample conversations
   - Edge case handling
   - Performance verification

## Post-Call Steps

1. **Review Configuration**
   - Check the setup summary
   - Verify key settings
   - Request adjustments if needed

2. **Initial Testing**
   - Test basic interactions
   - Verify personality settings
   - Check integration points

3. **Go Live Preparation**
   - Finalize any remaining settings
   - Set up monitoring
   - Prepare support team

## Best Practices

<Callout type="tip">
  Be specific about your needs and expectations during the setup call. The more detail you provide, the better the AI can configure your agent.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
  Don't rush through the setup call. Take the time to ensure everything is configured correctly for optimal performance.
</Callout>
